Honey Smoked Ham

Smoked with Hickory and Honey

Many years ago, bored with cooking in general and looking for a new low cost hobby, my old man decided to try smoking a ham one day. He purchased a smoker and decided that Hickory really was the way to go adding honey as a sweetener knowing that hickory can be earthy and bitter. That first attempt those many years ago lead to a renaissance of flavor and it was the most amazing ham my brother, sister, and I had ever eaten

Easy to make and to prepare as long as you have a smoker, this recipe drips with flavor and power, both sweet and also somehow bitter, both dry in some places and uncompromisingly moist in others, this ham recipe is the perfect complement to any Christmas, Easter, or Thanksgiving meal and is sure to cause family stories for many years to come

Ingredients

  • Spiral Cut Ham, or Normal Ham
  • Real Honey, mixes with water
  • Hickory Pellets, Chips, or Wood
  • Charcoal

Steps

  1. Prepare your smoker and add the Charcoal, lighting it and waiting until the edges of the charcoal turn white, this is to ensure the perfect temperature
  2. Once the smoker is prepared, unwrap the Ham and lay into a flat pan or bowl, taking the honey and applying liberally all over the ham and into the meat, its important to make sure the ham is completely covered to ensure proper flavor
  3. The smoker should be giving off heat now. Add your hickory and wait until the smoke becomes white, this ensures that the wood is smoking properly
  4. Finally add the Ham being careful to avoid the hot areas of the smoker and allow to smoke until the meat on the outside is dark brown but not burnt, there should be a glaze that appears full of flavor
  5. Finally, being careful to avoid the heat and wearing gloves if necessary, remove the ham and place into a clean and sanitized dish allowing it to cool. Your ham is now ready to eat and enjoy!
